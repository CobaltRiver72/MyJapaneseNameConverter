One Language, Four Writing Systems

Japanese is written with a mix of scripts. Kanji (漢字) are logographic characters that carry meaning, hiragana (ひらがな) is the curved kana syllabary used for native words and grammar, and katakana (カタカナ) is the angular kana syllabary used for loanwords, foreign names, and emphasis. Romaji (ローマ字) writes the same sounds in the Latin alphabet using Hepburn romanization, so learners can pronounce any word.

Because all four systems can express the same sounds, real Japanese text constantly moves between them — furigana readings above kanji, katakana forms of borrowed words, and romaji in textbooks and passports. That is exactly what these converter tools automate: give them Japanese text in one script, and they return it in another.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Japanese converter tool?
A Japanese converter tool changes text from one Japanese writing system into another — for example, kanji into hiragana readings, or hiragana into katakana. Every converter on this page runs instantly in your browser and shows romaji so you can pronounce the result.
Which Japanese converter should I use?
Use the Kanji to Hiragana Converter when you need the reading of kanji text, and the Hiragana to Katakana Converter when you need to switch between the two kana scripts. To write an English name in Japanese, use the Japanese Name Translator on our homepage.
What is the difference between kanji, hiragana, and katakana?
Kanji are logographic characters borrowed from Chinese that carry meaning, while hiragana and katakana are phonetic kana syllabaries. Hiragana is used for native Japanese words and grammar, and katakana is used for loanwords, foreign names, and emphasis.
